What Is Food Insecurity?

Food insecurity is an official term used by the United States Department of Agriculture (USDA) to describe a lack of access to safe and nutritious food to support a healthy and active lifestyle. Food insecurity has been most common, but not limited to, among low-income households and minority communities. In Los Angeles County, around 24% of households experience food insecurity. In recent years, due to aid from nutrition and resource programs, the trend among individuals experiencing food insecurity has decreased. However, Food insecurity remains a persistent challenge in many of our communities today. Addressing the issue requires strong community support, and by educating ourselves on the issue and being part of the solution, we can make a change that fosters a thriving future for our communities.
